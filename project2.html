Aggregate by Job Family

Next, I aggregated the data by their job family and year; I then summarized it to find the average and total salary by job family. In addition, I also generated variables n, which captures the number of observations for each job family, and Total Wealth encompasses all cash on the market. Once again, I checked for the structure of the data.

Job_fam <- compensation %>%
  group_by(Job.Family, Year)%>%
  summarise_at(vars(Salaries), list(sum=sum, mean = mean))%>%
  mutate(n = sum/mean)%>%
  mutate(total_wealth = sum(sum))%>%
  arrange(desc(mean))
str(Job_fam)

Analysis

As seen from the graph below. The average wage in these seven industries has increased with time. The Medical & Dental industry is the top three highest paying industries by average salary and has reached the highest average in San Francisco in 2021. In comparison, the Hospital Administration industry has the lowest average wage throughout the years.

It is also interesting to see how the number of workers in each industry fluctuates. For example, the Hospital Administration industry already has the lowest number of workers, yet it still saw a noticeable decrease in its workforce in 2021. It is essential to note that this decrease in the workforce is not unique to the Hospital Administration industry, as the overall workforce in these industries has decreased. The pandemic is likely the culprit behind the drop in the working population, as workers experienced higher burnout than they usually do.
